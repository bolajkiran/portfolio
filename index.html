About Me

I am a Master's thesis student in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Arlington. My area of interest include - Big Data, Distributed and Parallel Systems.

My current research is based on Graph mining and Multilayer Networks. One of the key areas of data mining research is developing efficient algorithms for finding frequent patterns in a large data set. To cope with large and complex types of objects that has multiple entities and feature types, the data is modelled as graphs and that is where graph mining come into picture. Graphs has variety of applications such as chemical, bioinformatics, computer vision, social networks, text retrieval and web analysis. Dealing with graph mining, one of the key problems is of finding frequent patterns, that is discovering subgraphs which occurs frequently or compress the graph in a better way over the entire graph. However, multilayer networks or MLNs (instead of attribute graphs) have been proven to be better for modeling complex data sets that have multiple entity and feature types. As a computer science researcher, I am involved in developing efficient algorithms for discovering substructures in complex attribute graphs and MLNs.

Hence, text categorization system is used to classify documents into different categories. Text categorization (also known as text classification or topics spotting) is the task of automatically sorting a set of documents into categories from a predefined set. Various learning techniques exist for the classification of text documents like Naïve Bayes, Support Vector Machine and Decision Trees, etc. The proposed system uses a Naive Bayesian method. In machine learning, naive Bayes classifiers are a family of simple probabilistic classifiers based on applying Bayes theorem with strong (naive) independence assumptions between the features. This is a web application project, where, an English text document is taken as input, then the proposed system should identify and categorise the text into category such as Sports, Politics, Entertainment, etc. in PHP, Bootstrap, MySQL.
